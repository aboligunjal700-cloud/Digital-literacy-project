# Phishing Attack Case Study

Phishing is a common cybercrime in which attackers try to trick people into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs by pretending to be a trusted source. These attacks usually happen through emails, messages, or fake websites.

In one scenario, a college student received an email that appeared to be from a well-known bank. The email stated that there was an issue with the student’s account and asked them to click a link to verify their details. The message looked official, with proper logos and formatting, which made it seem genuine. Without suspecting anything, the student clicked the link and was redirected to a fake website that looked exactly like the bank’s login page. The student entered their username, password, and OTP.
Within a few minutes, unauthorized transactions were made from the student’s bank account. The attacker had successfully captured the login details and gained access to the account.

This case shows how phishing attacks exploit trust and lack of awareness. Students and young users are often targeted because they may not carefully verify such messages. The consequences can include financial loss, identity theft, and compromised personal data.
Therefore, it is important to always verify the source of emails and avoid clicking on suspicious links.
